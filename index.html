Why nature is important?

Nature is crucial for the survival of all living beings, as it provides essential resources like clean air, water, food, and shelter. It regulates the climate, supports biodiversity, and offers vital ecosystem services such as pollination and water purification. Nature also contributes to mental and physical health, with outdoor environments promoting well-being. Furthermore, it has significant economic value through industries like agriculture, forestry, and tourism. Beyond its practical benefits, nature holds cultural and spiritual importance, inspiring creativity and offering spaces for reflection. Protecting nature ensures that future generations can continue to thrive and maintain the balance of life on Earth.

What is nature?

Nature refers to the natural world around us, encompassing all living organisms, plants, animals, and natural phenomena that exist without human intervention. It includes forests, rivers, mountains, oceans, and skies, as well as the weather and climate. Nature is a source of beauty, balance, and inspiration, offering a peaceful retreat from the stresses of modern life. It also plays a crucial role in sustaining life on Earth by providing essential resources like water, oxygen, and food. The preservation of nature is vital for maintaining ecological balance and supporting the well-being of all living beings.
